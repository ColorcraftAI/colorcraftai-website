The Right (and Wrong) Way to Ask Customers for Google Reviews in India

3 min read · February 2025 · By ColorcraftAI

Asking for reviews is one of the most powerful things you can do for your Google rating. Done correctly it is perfectly legal and highly effective. Done wrong, it can get your listing penalised. Here is what you need to know.

What Google actually says

Google's review policy is clear: you can ask customers to leave honest reviews. What you cannot do is incentivise them (offer discounts or gifts), pressure them, or only ask happy customers while filtering out unhappy ones. Review gating — only sending requests to customers you think are satisfied — is explicitly against Google's policy.

What works in India

The most effective review generation method for Indian businesses is a personal, post-experience WhatsApp message.

Do this

"Dr. Sharma here. Thank you for visiting us yesterday. If you have a moment, we would really appreciate an honest Google review — it helps other patients find us."

Not this

"Leave us a 5-star review and get 10% off your next visit!"

The best timing

Send your review request 24-48 hours after the visit — not immediately. The experience is fresh but the initial emotion has settled. For restaurants, the same evening works. For clinics, the following morning is ideal.

How many reviews do you need?

To rank well in local search in most Indian cities, you need a minimum of 25-30 reviews with a rating above 4.2 stars. To dominate a competitive category, you may need 50+ reviews at 4.5 stars or higher.
